Common Multi-Language SEO Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

❌ Mistake 1: Skipping Hreflang Tags

Problem: Google may penalize duplicate content if hreflang tags are missing. Solution: Always implement hreflang tags (self-referencing, paired, or grouped).

❌ Mistake 2: Using Machine Translation Without Editing

Problem: AI translations can sound robotic and hurt UX. Solution: Use AI as a starting point, then human-review key pages.

❌ Mistake 3: Ignoring Local Search Intent

Problem: English keywords don’t work in other languages. Solution: Use AI tools to find local search intent (e.g., "meilleur" in French vs. "best" in English).

❌ Mistake 4: Not Optimizing for Mobile in Local Markets

Problem: Mobile traffic in some countries (e.g., India, Brazil) is 90%+. Solution: Test with Google’s Mobile-Friendly Test and optimize for local mobile users.

❌ Mistake 5: Overlooking Local Business Schema

Problem: Missing LocalBusiness schema hurts local pack rankings. Solution: Auto-generate schema for address, phone, hours in target languages.
