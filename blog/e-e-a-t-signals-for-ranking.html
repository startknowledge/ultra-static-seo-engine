What Is E-E-A-T? The Full Breakdown

Before diving into optimization strategies, let’s clarify what E-E-A-T actually means—because misconceptions abound.

1. The Original E-A-T (Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)

Google’s 2014 Quality Rater Guidelines introduced E-A-T, focusing on:

  • Expertise – Does the content reflect real-world knowledge?
  • Authoritativeness – Is the source widely recognized as a leader in its field?
  • Trustworthiness – Can users rely on the information?

2. The Addition of "Experience" (E-E-A-T)

In 2022, Google expanded the framework to E-E-A-T, emphasizing firsthand experience—especially for Your Money or Your Life (YMYL) topics (health, finance, legal, etc.).

Why the change? Google wants to ensure that people get accurate, practical advice—not just theoretical knowledge. For example:

  • A doctor’s blog on diabetes management carries more weight than a general wellness site unless the author has direct patient experience.
  • A financial advisor’s guide on retirement planning is more trustworthy if the author has decades of hands-on experience rather than just a certification.

3. How Google Evaluates E-E-A-T (According to Insiders)

While Google doesn’t disclose exact ranking factors, search quality evaluators (the humans who rate pages) use subjective and objective signals to assess E-E-A-T. These include:

  • Author bios (Are they credible?)
  • Backlinks from authoritative sources (Are they cited by trusted sites?)
  • User engagement metrics (Do people trust and share the content?)
  • Content depth & accuracy (Is it well-researched?)
  • Brand reputation (Are there negative reviews or lawsuits?)

Key Takeaway: E-E-A-T isn’t just about keywords or backlinks—it’s about proving your site is a reliable source in your niche.

The 10 Most Impactful E-E-A-T Signals for Ranking in 2024

Now that we understand what E-E-A-T is, let’s explore the most actionable signals you can optimize for today.

1. Author Credibility: The "Who Wrote This?" Factor

Why it matters: Google’s 2022 update made it clear—content without clear authorship is less trustworthy. If users can’t verify who created the content, they (and Google) may question its expertise and reliability.

How to Optimize for Author Credibility

✔ Use Real, Named Authors (Not "The Team" or "John Doe")

  • Example: Instead of "Our SEO experts wrote this," use:

    "This guide was written by Sarah Johnson, a certified SEO consultant with 10+ years in technical SEO and a Google Analytics Certified Professional."

✔ Include Detailed Author Bios (With Verifiable Credentials)

  • Bad: "Sarah is an SEO specialist."
  • Good:

    "Sarah Johnson holds a Master’s in Digital Marketing from the University of Michigan and has consulted Fortune 500 companies on SEO strategy. She’s also a speaker at SMX and MozCon."

✔ Link to Professional Profiles (LinkedIn, Personal Website, Certifications)

  • Example:

    "For more on Sarah’s expertise, visit her LinkedIn or her personal SEO blog."

✔ Use "About the Author" Sections (With a Photo & Social Proof)

  • Example:

    "Sarah has been featured in Search Engine Journal and HubSpot for her work in AI-driven SEO strategies."

Real-World Example: How HubSpot Uses Author Credibility

HubSpot’s blog posts always include:

  • Author name + photo
  • Job title + company affiliation
  • LinkedIn profile link
  • A short bio with credentials

This instantly builds trust because readers can verify the author’s expertise before consuming the content.

2. Content Depth & Accuracy: The "Is This Actually Useful?" Test

Why it matters: Google’s Helpful Content Update (2022) penalized thin, low-effort content. If your page doesn’t solve a problem deeply, it won’t rank—no matter how many backlinks you have.

How to Optimize for Depth & Accuracy

✔ Answer Questions Fully (Not Just Skim the Surface)

  • Bad: "SEO is important for rankings."
  • Good:

    *"SEO (Search Engine Optimization) isn’t just about keywords—it’s a multi-layered strategy that includes technical SEO (site speed, mobile-friendliness), on-page SEO (headers, meta tags), and off-page SEO (backlinks, brand mentions). In fact, *sites with optimized technical SEO rank 3x faster in Google (per a 2023 Backlinko study)."

✔ Cite Sources (Studies, Expert Interviews, Official Documents)

  • Example:

    "According to Google’s 2024 Search Quality Rater Guidelines, E-E-A-T is now a top-3 ranking factor for YMYL topics."

✔ Update Old Content (Google favors fresh, accurate information)

  • Tool Tip: Use Ahrefs’ Content Explorer to find outdated posts in your niche and improve them.

✔ Include Case Studies & Real Data (Not Just Opinions)

  • Example:

    *"We tested 10 different SEO strategies over 6 months and found that internal linking increased organic traffic by 47%—here’s how we did it."*

Real-World Example: How Backlinko Dominates with Depth

Brian Dean’s Backlinko posts are legendary because they:

  • Start with a bold claim (e.g., "This one SEO trick increased my traffic by 110%").
  • Prove it with data (e.g., "Here’s the exact experiment we ran").
  • Break it down step-by-step (with screenshots, code snippets, and actionable takeaways).

This depth makes his content unmatched in trustworthiness.

3. Backlinks from Authoritative Sources: The "Who Links to You?" Signal

Why it matters: Backlinks are still one of the strongest E-A-T signals—but not all links are equal. A link from a .edu or .gov site carries far more weight than a spammy forum.

How to Get High-Quality Backlinks

✔ Guest Post on Trusted Sites (But Only Where It Fits)

  • Example:
    • Bad: Writing for a low-authority blog just for links.
    • Good: Contributing to Moz Blog, Search Engine Journal, or industry-specific publications.

✔ Get Featured in Expert Roundups

  • Example:

    "We asked 20 SEO experts: ‘What’s the #1 SEO mistake in 2024?’ Here’s what they said."

✔ Leverage HARO (Help a Reporter Out)

  • Journalists often need expert quotes—if you provide valuable insights, they’ll link back.

✔ Create Link-Worthy Content (Studies, Tools, Unique Data)

  • Example:
    • "We ran a 6-month experiment on AI vs. human SEO—here’s what we found."

Real-World Example: How Neil Patel Builds Backlinks

Neil Patel’s content often gets links because:

  • He publishes original research (e.g., "The Ultimate Guide to AI in Marketing").
  • He gets quoted in major outlets (Forbes, Entrepreneur).
  • He collaborates with other experts (e.g., "50 SEO Experts Share Their Biggest Mistakes").

Pro Tip: Use Ahrefs’ "Content Gap" tool to find where competitors get links and replicate (or surpass) their strategies.

4. User Engagement Signals: The "Do People Trust This?" Metric

Why it matters: Google uses dwell time, bounce rate, and social shares to gauge how users perceive your content. If people leave quickly, Google assumes the content is untrustworthy.

How to Improve User Engagement for E-E-A-T

✔ Write for Real People, Not Just Search Engines

  • Example:
    • Bad: "This guide explains how to do SEO."
    • Good: "Struggling with low rankings? Here’s the exact step-by-step SEO strategy we used to increase organic traffic by 300% in 3 months."

✔ Use Subheadings, Bullet Points, and Visuals (Even If You Can’t Add Images)

  • Example:

    Step 1: Keyword Research

    • Use Ahrefs to find low-competition, high-intent keywords.
    • Focus on long-tail queries (e.g., "best SEO tools for small businesses").

✔ Encourage Comments & Discussions

  • Example:

    "What’s your biggest SEO challenge right now? Drop a comment below—we’ll answer!"

✔ Monitor & Fix High Bounce Rates

  • Tool Tip: Use Google Analytics 4 to see where users drop off and improve those sections.

Real-World Example: How BuzzSumo Engages Readers

BuzzSumo’s content often includes:

  • Interactive elements (e.g., "Which social media platform drives the most traffic?").
  • Polls & surveys (e.g., "What’s your biggest content marketing struggle?").
  • Actionable templates (e.g., "Free SEO Audit Checklist").

This keeps users engaged longer, signaling to Google that the content is valuable.

5. Brand Reputation & Reviews: The "What Do Others Say?" Factor

Why it matters: If your brand has negative reviews, lawsuits, or shady practices, Google will penalize you. Even one bad review can hurt your E-A-T.

How to Build a Strong Brand Reputation

✔ Encourage & Respond to Reviews (Google My Business, Trustpilot, etc.)

  • Example:

    "We’re sorry to hear about your experience! We’ve addressed this issue—here’s how we fixed it."

✔ Monitor Online Mentions (Use Google Alerts, Mention, or Brand24)

  • Example:
    • If someone complains about your product, respond publicly (if appropriate).

✔ Avoid Controversial Stances (Unless You’re a Thought Leader)

  • Example:
    • Bad: "AI is overrated—stick to manual SEO."
    • Good: "AI can boost SEO efficiency, but human oversight is still critical—here’s how to use both."

✔ Get Featured in Trusted Directories (BBB, Yelp, Chamber of Commerce)

  • Example:
    • "We’re an Accredited Business with the Better Business Bureau."

Real-World Example: How Amazon Maintains Trust

Amazon:

  • Has a strict review policy (removing fake reviews).
  • Responds to customer complaints publicly.
  • Uses AI to detect fraudulent activity.

This transparency makes Amazon one of the most trusted e-commerce brands.

6. Content Freshness: The "Is This Still Relevant?" Signal

Why it matters: Google prefers updated content—especially in fast-changing industries (tech, finance, health).

How to Keep Content Fresh

✔ Update Old Posts Annually (Add new stats, examples, or case studies)

  • Example:

    "We last updated this post in 2022—here’s what’s new in 2024."

✔ Add a "Last Updated" Timestamp

  • Example:

    "This guide was last updated on May 15, 2024."

✔ Use Google’s "Update" Tag (For News & Time-Sensitive Topics)

  • Example:

    "This article was last reviewed by our health expert team on June 2024."

✔ Publish "Evergreen" Updates (New sections on trending topics)

  • Example:

    "Since AI tools like ChatGPT entered the market, we’ve added a new section on AI SEO tools."

Real-World Example: How Wikipedia Stays Updated

Wikipedia:

  • Has a clear "Last Edited" date.
  • Encourages community edits (but vets contributors).
  • Removes outdated information quickly.

This keeps it the most trusted source on the web.
