The Role of AI in Modern SEO: A 2024 Perspective

AI isn’t replacing human writers—it’s augmenting them. According to Ahrefs’ 2024 Content Marketing Report, AI-assisted content creation helps marketers:

  • Reduce content production time by 70% (vs. manual writing)
  • Improve keyword optimization with real-time SEO suggestions
  • Generate multiple content variations (e.g., blog posts, social media snippets, email copy)
  • Detect content gaps using AI-powered SEO tools (e.g., SurferSEO, Clearscope)

However, Google’s 2024 algorithm updates (like March 2024’s helpful content update) still prioritize human-curated, high-value content. This means AI-generated content must be: ✔ Well-researched (not just regurgitated) ✔ Structured for readability (clear headings, bullet points, scannable content) ✔ Optimized for intent (not just keywords) ✔ Enhanced with human oversight (editing, fact-checking, brand alignment)

8 Actionable Strategies for Generating AI Content That Ranks

Now that we’ve covered the foundations, let’s dive into practical, high-impact strategies to generate AI content that dominates search rankings.

Strategy 1: Use AI for Keyword Research & Topic Ideation (Before Writing)

Problem: Many marketers jump straight to writing without proper keyword research, leading to low-traffic, irrelevant content.

Solution: AI can uncover high-potential keywords and topic clusters before you even draft a single word.

How to Do It:

  1. Use AI tools for keyword expansion (e.g., SurferSEO + Jasper AI, Clearscope + Copy.ai)
    • Example: If you’re writing about "best AI tools for small businesses," an AI tool can suggest long-tail variations like:
      • "Affordable AI tools for startups in 2024"
      • "Best free AI writing assistants for bloggers"
      • "How AI can automate customer support for SMBs"
  2. Analyze competitor content with AI-powered tools like Ahrefs’ Content Gap Analysis
    • AI can identify gaps in competitors’ content and suggest better angles.
  3. Leverage AI for question-based content (People Also Ask sections)
    • Tools like AnswerThePublic (now AI-enhanced) can generate FAQ-style questions that trigger rich snippets.

Real-World Example: HubSpot uses AI (via HubSpot’s AI Content Assistant) to discover trending topics in their niche. For instance, when they noticed a surge in searches for "AI for sales automation," they generated a detailed blog series covering:

  • "How AI Can Boost Your Sales Pipeline in 2024"
  • "Top 10 AI Tools for Sales Teams (Free & Paid)"
  • "Case Study: How [Company] Used AI to Close 30% More Deals"

Result: Their AI sales automation content ranked #1 on Google within two weeks due to high search intent and structured formatting.

Strategy 2: Generate AI-Drafted Outlines for Maximum SEO Value

Problem: Even if AI writes a decent draft, poor structure kills engagement and rankings.

Solution: AI can create optimized outlines before writing a single sentence.

How to Do It:

  1. Use AI to generate a high-level outline (e.g., Jasper AI’s "Outline" feature)
    • Example prompt:

      "Create a detailed outline for a 2,000-word blog post on 'How to Use AI for Local SEO in 2024.' Include H2, H3 subheadings, and keyword placements for 'AI local SEO,' 'Google Business Profile optimization,' and 'AI-generated reviews.'"

  2. Ensure the outline follows the Skyscraper Technique (better, deeper, more comprehensive than competitors)
    • AI can compare top-ranking pages and suggest missing sections.
  3. Use AI to draft FAQ sections (which trigger rich snippets)
    • Example:
      • "Can AI improve my Google My Business ranking?"
      • "What’s the best AI tool for local business reviews?"

Real-World Example: Backlinko (Brian Dean’s site) uses AI to structure their guides before writing. For their "Ultimate Guide to Local SEO", they:

  • Used AI to identify that competitors missed sections on "AI-powered citation building."
  • Structured the post with H2s like:
    • "How AI Can Automate Google Business Profile Updates"
    • "AI Tools for Scraping Local Business Directories"
  • Result: Their updated guide ranked #1 for "local SEO 2024" within three days.

Strategy 3: Write AI-Generated Drafts & Human-Refine for Brand Voice

Problem: Pure AI-generated content lacks brand personality and can sound generic.

Solution: AI drafts + human refinement = the best of both worlds.

How to Do It:

  1. Generate a first draft using Jasper, Copy.ai, or Google’s Bard
    • Example prompt:

      "Write a 1,500-word guide on 'How AI Can Help Small Businesses Save Time in 2024.' Use a conversational tone, include case studies, and optimize for the keyword 'AI productivity tools for SMBs.'"

  2. Run the draft through Grammarly Business or Hemingway Editor to improve readability
  3. Add human insights (e.g., real customer stories, industry expertise)
  4. Optimize for E-E-A-T by:
    • Adding author bios (if applicable)
    • Citing reputable sources (AI may not always fact-check)
    • Including expert quotes (via AI-powered interview tools like Otter.ai)

Real-World Example: Neil Patel’s blog uses AI for initial drafts but refines them with human expertise. For their post "How AI Can Improve Your Email Marketing," they:

  • Let AI generate a rough draft with basic structure and keyword placement.
  • Had their in-house marketers add:
    • Real case studies (e.g., "How [Client] Increased Open Rates by 40% with AI-Powered Subject Lines")
    • Expert opinions from their email marketing consultants
  • Result: The post ranked #3 on Google for "AI email marketing" within a week.

Strategy 4: Use AI for Repurposing Content Across Formats (Ultra-Static SEO)

Problem: Creating new content from scratch is time-consuming. Repurposing old content can lead to duplicate content penalties.

Solution: AI can transform one piece of content into multiple formats while keeping SEO value intact.

How to Do It:

  1. Turn a blog post into:
    • A LinkedIn carousel (using AI-generated visual descriptions)
    • A YouTube script (with AI voiceover suggestions)
    • An infographic outline (for tools like Canva)
    • A Twitter thread (with AI-optimized hooks)
  2. Use AI to update old content with new data
    • Example: If you have a 2022 blog post on "AI in Marketing," use AI to:
      • Add 2024 statistics (via Statista, SEMrush)
      • Update case studies with new examples
      • Refresh keyword optimization (using Ahrefs’ Content Explorer)
  3. Generate AI-powered summaries for social media
    • Example:

      "Just published: 'How AI Can 10X Your Productivity in 2024' 🚀 🔹 Save 10+ hours/week with AI tools 🔹 Real case studies from top SMBs 🔹 Free checklist inside! #AI #Productivity"

Real-World Example: Buffer (a social media management tool) repurposed their "Social Media Trends 2023" blog into:

  • A LinkedIn video series (using AI-generated captions)
  • A Twitter thread (with AI-optimized engagement hooks)
  • An email newsletter (with AI-personalized subject lines)
  • A YouTube Shorts script (using AI voiceover suggestions)

Result: Their content reached 50% more audiences with minimal extra effort, while keeping SEO rankings stable.

Strategy 5: Optimize AI Content for Featured Snippets & Voice Search

Problem: Most AI-generated content misses featured snippets, which drive 30% of all search traffic (per Ahrefs).

Solution: AI can structure content for snippet eligibility and voice search optimization.

How to Do It:

  1. Use AI to identify snippet-worthy questions
    • Example: For "How to use AI for local SEO," AI can suggest:
      • "What are the top 5 AI tools for local SEO in 2024?"
      • "How does AI improve Google Business Profile rankings?"
  2. Structure content in bullet points, tables, and numbered lists (snippet-friendly)
    • Example:

      Top 5 AI Tools for Local SEO (2024)

      1. BrightLocal – AI-powered citation builder
      2. SE Ranking – Local keyword tracking with AI insights
      3. AnswerThePublic – Question-based content generator
      4. SurferSEO – AI content optimization for local rankings
      5. Google’s Vertex AI – Custom local search algorithms
  3. Optimize for voice search (long-tail, conversational queries)
    • Example:

      "What’s the best AI tool for small business reviews?" AI-generated answer: "The best AI tool for small business reviews is Podium, which uses natural language processing to generate authentic customer feedback. It also integrates with Google Business Profile for seamless updates."

Real-World Example: Backlinko’s "How to Rank on Google" guide became a featured snippet machine because:

  • They used AI to identify that competitors lacked structured lists.
  • They formatted answers in bullet points (e.g., "10 Steps to Rank #1 in 2024").
  • They optimized for voice search by answering common questions like:

    "How long does it take to rank on Google?" "What’s the best backlink strategy in 2024?"

Result: Their guide appears in the featured snippet for 15+ keywords, driving thousands of clicks monthly.

Problem: Static content (like blogs) doesn’t adapt to user intent over time.

Solution: AI can dynamically adjust content based on user behavior, location, and search intent.

      "Top 3 AI Tools for Chicago SMBs (2024)

      • HubSpot (Chicago HQ) – Best for CRM automation
